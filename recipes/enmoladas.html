Enmoladas

Description

Tamales smothered in pumpkin mole

Ingredients

  • 1 White Onion
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • 6 Ancho Chiles
  • 3 Cloves
  • 5 Allspice Berries
  • 1/4 Cup Roasted Peanuts
  • 1/2 Tsp Ground Canela Mexicana
  • 2 Cups Roasted Pumpkin (Pureed)
  • 3 Cups Vegetable Stock
  • 1 Small Piloncillo
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Sea Salt
  • 2 tbsp Coconut Oil
  • 12-18 Corn Tortillas
  • 2 Cups Diced Pumpkin
  • 1 Pickled Red Onion
  • 1/4 Cilantro Leaves

Preparation (Mole)

  1. Toast onions and garlic on medium heat
  2. Cut end of garlic and remove cloves
  3. Toast ancho chiles for 1 minute per side
  4. Submerge chiles in boiled water for 30 minutes
  5. Drain and save broth
  6. Remove and discard seeds and stems from chiles
  7. Put chiles in blender
  8. Toast cloves and allspice berries for 1 minute or until fragrant
  9. Puree onions garlic, chiles, 1/2 cup chile broth, cloves, allspice, peanuts, cinnamon, pumpkin, stock, piloncillo, and salt in a high-speed blender
    *If high-speed blender is not available, dissolve piloncillo in 1 cup of stock before adding to blender
  10. Cook puree for 20 minutes on medium heat, stir occasionally

Preparation (Enchiladas)

  1. Preheat oven to 350F (180C)
  2. Pour mole into 9in x 13in baking dish
  3. Heat tlaxcalli on meadium heat
  4. Dip tlaxcalli in mole
  5. Fill tlaxcalli with preferred filling (e.g. meat, pumpkin, etc.)
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 until baking dish is full
  7. Pour 1 cup of mole over rolled enmoladas
  8. Sprinkle with remainder of cheese, cover in foil, and bake for 20 minutes
  9. Remove foil and bake for 5 more minutes
  10. Garnish with pickled red onions and cilantro leaves, or as desired
