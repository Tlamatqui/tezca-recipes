Birria

Description

Birria is a popular Mexican dish originating from Jalisco, Mexico. Though traditionally made with lamb, beef has become popular due to lower cost and high supply.

Birria is typically enjoyed shredded in consome, a soup made with dried chiles and other spices, or shredded into tacos and quesadillas after cooking in consome.

Ingredients (Meat)

  • 1 lb Bone-in Lamb Ribs, Beef, Shank, or Beef Loin
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Salt
  • 1 Tbsp Pepper
  • 1 Tsp Ground Cumin

Ingredients (Consome)

  • 4 Ancho Chiles
  • 4 Guajillo Chiles
  • 1 Chipotle Pepper
  • 8 Peppercorns
  • 2 Cloves
  • 1 Tsp Dried Thyme
  • 1 Tsb Marjoram
  • 1 Tsp Dried Oregano
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1/2 Tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1/2 Tsp Ground Ginger
  • 1/2 Cinnamon Stick
  • 8 Garlic Cloves
  • 1/2 Red Onion
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Salt
  • 4 Tomatoes
  • 1/4 Cup Apple Cider Vinegar
  • 2 Cups Water

Preparation

  1. season meat with salt, pepper, and cumin
  2. Remove seeds from chiles
  3. Fry chiles with small amount of oil for 2-3 minutes and set aside
  4. Boil chiles for 10 minutes (or until soft)
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of oil and brown the onions and tomatoes
  6. Add garlic peppercorns, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and remaining spices (except ginger and ground cumin)
  7. Stir for 5 minutes
  8. Blend chiles and broth, ginger, cumin, apple cider vineger, and water until smooth
  9. Marinate meat for at least 2 hours (overnight preffered)
  10. Add meat and marinade to large stockpot and cook over medium heat, covered, for about three hours (or until meat is tender)
  11. Shred meat and serve with consome or in tacos
